The Website I Should Never Have Created

It was the middle of the night.
The digital clock on the bedside table read exactly 3:00.
The room was immersed in that eerie silence that seems to scream. Every little noise—the buzzing of the computer, the clicking of the mouse, even my own breathing—was too loud.

I was there, in front of the monitor, creating a horror website. I didn't want it to be a normal website: I wanted something that would truly disturb anyone who visited it. A portal that wasn't just read, but experienced.
I wanted to scare others.
I didn't think the website would scare me.

The code flowed. But suddenly the website began to write itself. Texts appeared without me touching the keyboard.
Short sentences. Direct.

"Stop it."
"You're not the author."
"We see you."

The forbidden folder

I checked the server files.
At the bottom, I found a folder I didn't remember creating: YOU.

Inside, a single image.

I opened it.

It was my room. Identical.
The perspective was that of someone watching me in real time. The monitor was on, my chair... and in the corner, next to the closet, a dark figure, tall, motionless.

I turned sharply. The corner was empty.

I looked at the image again.
The figure was closer.

I deleted the file, but the folder immediately reappeared. Inside, a new photo.
The figure was now next to the chair. My chair.

The Message

Suddenly, the monitor flashed.
A black window appeared with red writing:

"You are not the author. You are the character."

I heard a click: my webcam had activated.
The screen showed the room... but it wasn't showing me.
It was showing behind me.

A cold shiver paralyzed me. I couldn't move a muscle.
Then I felt a slow, deep breath behind my neck.
A bony, icy hand grabbed my shoulder.

The Transformation

The site loaded a final page all by itself.
Black background.
In the center, a red text that pulsed like living flesh:

"You are now part of the site."

The screen changed. In place of the words, my face appeared, but it was no longer mine. It was pale, distorted, with hollow eyes and an unnatural smile.
I tried to scream, but on the screen, my image spoke for me. The lips moved slowly, and the whisper resonated directly in my head:

"Welcome home."

The Fourth Wall

I thought that was the end of it.
But when I tried to tell this story, something changed.

Because every time I write it, every time someone reads it, the site recreates itself.
I'm not writing the words you see now.
They are.

And in fact... tell me something.
Look carefully at the screen.

Don't you think the light in your room is a little different than it was a moment ago?
Don't you have the feeling there's someone behind you, just beyond the edge of the monitor?

Don't turn around right away.

Wait.

In a moment, you'll see them reflected in the screen, just as I saw them.
And when that happens... the YOU folder will already be on your computer.
