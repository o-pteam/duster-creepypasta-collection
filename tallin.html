The 3:17 AM Call


Her name was Tallin. She was a quiet girl, with the habit of leaving her cell phone on all night, on the nightstand, with the ringer on full blast. She said it made her feel safer, "always reachable."

One night she woke up suddenly: her phone was ringing. She checked the time: 3:17 AM.
On the display, an unfamiliar number. Uncertain, but curious, she answered.

There was no voice on the other end. Just slow, almost unnatural breathing, filling the earpiece as if someone were too close to the microphone.
"Hello?" Tallin said, her voice faint.

Silence. Then, a cold whisper:
"Don't look out the window."

A shiver ran down her spine. Despite the warning, her eyes moved on their own. She looked.

The curtains were slightly open, and between the cracks was something. A tall, motionless figure seemed to be staring at her. She couldn't make out a face, but she felt that gaze upon her.

She yanked the curtains shut and curled up under the covers, her heart racing. The phone hadn't stopped transmitting that breathing.

Then the voice came back, closer, more cruel:
"You looked."

The line went dead.

The next morning, she checked her call log: no incoming numbers, no trace of the call.

From that night on, every day at 3:17 a.m., her phone vibrated. And the same message always appeared on the screen:
"I'm still at your window."
