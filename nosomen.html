"Don't turn off the light, Gabrydabry"

Gabrydabry wasn't a particularly brave boy.
He was sixteen, living in a small town where the sky was always cloudy and the streets at night seemed like empty tunnels dug into nothingness.
But what really tormented him... was the darkness.

Not a simple fear.
Not the childish fear of the monster under the bed.
But a deep-rooted, visceral phobia, as if the darkness were alive and waiting for him.

Every night he left all the lights on in the house. Lamps, bedside lamps, oven lights, even the phone flashlight.
His father yelled about the bill. His mother tried to reassure him.
But Gabrydabry couldn't explain... because he didn't really know what was terrifying him either.

Then one night, something happened.

It was 2:13 in the morning. He remembered it well, because he had looked at the digital clock at least ten times in the last few minutes.
The wind outside rattled the windows. The house seemed to breathe.

And then…
Click.

The light went out.

All the lights.

In an instant, the darkness swallowed him like a wave of ink.

His breath caught in his throat.

He jumped out of bed, groping blindly along the wall for the light switch.
But something had changed.

The walls seemed farther away.

The room… was no longer his.

A whisper.
He heard it distinctly, even though it didn't seem to come from outside, but from within him.

 

> "Why are you afraid, Gabrydabry?"

 

He whirled around, but saw nothing.
And yet… there was something. A presence.
Something moving in the darkness, but making no sound.
Only that breathing… deep, cavernous, too close.

 

> “You’ve been turning on the lights for years.


But I was always there.
Just a step away.”

Something touched his shoulder. Cold.
Gabrydabry screamed, stumbled, fell to the floor.

Then a light came on. A small, flickering one.
It came from a mirror he’d never seen before, set in the wall.
Inside the mirror, he didn’t see himself.

He saw a room identical to his own, but with someone sitting on the bed.
A boy.
With his same face.
But with eyes… completely black.

 

> “Are you me?” whispered Gabrydabry.

 

> “No,” the creature replied, smiling.


“I am the you you left in the dark.”

And the mirror exploded.

Gabrydabry woke up the next morning.
The lights were back on. His room was normal again.
His parents said they'd had a blackout.

But since that day, every time he closes his eyes...
he still sees that version of himself, there in the darkness, smiling at him.

And every night, at 2:13, all the lights flicker.

Just for a moment.

But it's enough to remind him.
