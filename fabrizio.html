"Massacre Island" – The Story Within the Story

Fabrizio was twenty years old and lived alone in a small apartment on the outskirts of the city. He didn't have many friends, but he didn't care: he preferred spending his nights on the computer, writing his horror story on Wattpad.

He called it "Massacre Island." It was his obsession. Every night he added new details, new deaths, new dark presences. The more he wrote, the more he felt something watching him. But he thought it was just a hunch. When you live alone, even silence seems like noise.

That night, however, something was different.

He closed his laptop at 3:17 AM. He rubbed his eyes, huffed, and threw himself onto the bed, exhausted. He fell asleep almost immediately.

But he wasn't dreaming.

---

He woke up. Or at least, that's what it seemed like. He couldn't move. He was lying on the bed, his eyes wide, his body rigid. He tried to scream, but his mouth was sealed by an invisible silence. Sleep paralysis, he thought… He'd heard about it.

Then he felt the wind.

A cold breath caressed the back of his neck, as if someone were breathing right behind him.

But the window was closed. The door was closed. There was no way to let air in.

And yet the wind grew stronger. Until it became a hiss. A deep breath. Uneven.

He wasn't alone.

Slowly, with the extreme effort of someone imprisoned in his own body, he barely managed to move his eyes… and he saw it.

Behind him, standing on the bed, was a being.

It was tall. Too tall to be human. Its neck was long, twisted like a broken branch. It slid down toward him like a snake hanging from the ceiling. Its mouth was wide open, motionless, as if screaming but making no sound. Its eyes were empty sockets, but they were weeping blood. Not just one or two trickles: it was a constant flow, as if the creature were emptying its soul through its eyes.

And it was staring at him. From above, its neck bent at an unnatural angle.

It wasn't moving. It wasn't breathing. But it was there.

Fabrizio felt his heart pounding in his chest. He wanted to wake up. He wanted to scream. But it was as if the monster itself were part of the dream... or reality.

Then, finally, he managed to close his eyes for a second. When he opened them again... it was gone.
